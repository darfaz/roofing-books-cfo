## Purpose

Ensure every crew member is aware of daily hazards and safety requirements before work begins, reducing workplace injuries and maintaining OSHA compliance.

## Scope

All roofing work sites. Safety briefings are mandatory for every crew member, every day, before any work begins.

## OSHA Requirements

- 29 CFR 1926.21 - Safety training and education
- 29 CFR 1926.501 - Fall protection (6 feet in residential)
- 29 CFR 1926.1053 - Ladders
- 29 CFR 1926.451 - Scaffolding (if used)

## Procedure

### 1. Briefing Requirements

**When:** Every day, before work starts
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**Who Leads:** Crew Lead
**Who Attends:** ALL workers on site that day
**Documentation:** Signed attendance sheet

### 2. Daily Safety Briefing Agenda

**2.1 Roll Call and Fitness Check**
- Verify all workers present
- Confirm everyone is fit for duty
- Ask: "Is anyone feeling unwell, fatigued, or impaired?"

**2.2 Job Site Overview**
- Address and customer name
- Type of work today
- Estimated scope/duration
- Any customer special requirements

**2.3 Weather Conditions**
- Current temperature
- Forecast for the day
- Wind conditions
- Rain probability
- Heat/cold protocols if applicable

**2.4 Hazard Identification**
Walk through site-specific hazards:
- [ ] Power lines and utilities
- [ ] Roof pitch and fall protection needs
- [ ] Soft or damaged deck areas
- [ ] Trees or overhead obstacles
- [ ] Ground conditions
- [ ] Adjacent property concerns
- [ ] Traffic/pedestrian exposure
- [ ] Animals or insects

**2.5 Fall Protection Review**
Based on today's work:
- Fall protection method in use
- Anchor point locations
- Equipment inspection status
- Rescue plan reminder

**2.6 PPE Check**
Verify everyone has:
- [ ] Hard hat (when required)
- [ ] Safety glasses
- [ ] Work gloves
- [ ] Proper footwear
- [ ] Fall protection gear (if needed)
- [ ] High-visibility vest (if needed)

**2.7 Emergency Procedures**
Review/remind:
- First aid kit location
- Fire extinguisher location
- Emergency contact numbers
- Nearest hospital/urgent care
- Assembly point if evacuation needed

**2.8 Questions and Concerns**
- "Does anyone have questions?"
- "Does anyone see a hazard I missed?"
- "Does anyone have concerns about today's work?"

### 4. Special Condition Protocols

**4.1 High Heat (Over 90°F)**
- Start work early (6-7 AM)
- Mandatory breaks every hour
- Shade/cooling area required
- Water available at all times
- Watch for heat illness signs
- Consider ending work by 2 PM

**4.2 Cold Weather (Under 40°F)**
- Warm-up breaks scheduled
- Shingle handling precautions
- Watch for ice/frost
- Proper cold weather clothing

**4.3 High Wind (Over 15 mph)**
- Enhanced caution on roof
- Secure all materials
- Consider stopping if over 25 mph
- No lifting of sheet materials

**4.4 Wet/Slippery Conditions**
- Assess roof walkability
- Enhanced grip footwear
- Extra caution required
- Consider delay if unsafe

### 5. Incident Reporting Reminder

During briefing, remind crew:
```
"If anyone gets hurt - even a minor injury -
report it immediately. Don't wait until end
of day. We want to take care of you and
prevent it from happening again."
```

### 6. Documentation Requirements

**6.1 Briefing Form Storage**
- Keep original in job file
- Maintain for minimum 3 years
- Available for OSHA inspection

**6.2 Photo Documentation**
Take photo of:
- Signed briefing form
- Site hazards discussed
- PPE being used

### 7. Abbreviated Briefing (Same Site, Day 2+)

If continuing same job:
- Review any changes from yesterday
- Note any new hazards
- Weather update
- PPE check
- Still requires signature

### 8. Training Records

Track training completed:
- Annual OSHA 10-hour training
- Fall protection training
- Equipment-specific training
- First aid/CPR certification
