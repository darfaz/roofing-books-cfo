```
Document #: CS-001
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: [DATE]
Last Reviewed: [DATE]

```

## Purpose

Resolve customer complaints quickly and professionally, turning dissatisfied customers into loyal advocates while protecting company reputation.

## Scope

All customer complaints received through any channel: phone, email, online reviews, social media, or in person.

## Core Principles

1. **Listen First** - Understand before responding
2. **Own It** - Take responsibility, don't make excuses
3. **Act Fast** - Speed of response matters
4. **Resolve Completely** - Fix the issue AND the relationship
5. **Learn** - Every complaint is improvement opportunity

## Complaint Categories

| Category | Examples | Response Time |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Safety | Active leak, dangerous condition | Immediate (same day) |
| Quality | Workmanship issue, defective work | 24 hours |
| Service | Communication, scheduling, cleanup | 24-48 hours |
| Billing | Invoice dispute, overcharge | 24-48 hours |
| Warranty | Covered repair needed | 48-72 hours |

## Procedure

### 1. Initial Complaint Receipt

**1.1 Capture Complete Information**
- [ ] Customer name and contact info
- [ ] Property address (if different)
- [ ] Date of service
- [ ] Nature of complaint (in their words)
- [ ] How they contacted us
- [ ] Their desired resolution (if stated)
- [ ] Emotional state assessment

**1.2 Complaint Log Entry**
```
COMPLAINT LOG
=============
Date/Time Received: ________________
Received By: ______________________
Customer: _________________________
Phone: ___________________________
Address: __________________________
Job Date: ________________________
Complaint Summary:
_______________________________________
_______________________________________
Customer's Desired Resolution:
_______________________________________
Initial Response By: ___________ Date: ___
```

**1.3 Immediate Acknowledgment**
Within 2 hours (during business hours):
```
Phone: "Thank you for letting us know. I'm sorry you're
experiencing this issue. Let me get some information so
we can take care of this right away."

Email: "Thank you for bringing this to our attention.
We take this seriously and are already working on it.
Someone will contact you within [timeframe]."
```

### 2. Investigation

**2.1 Review History**
- Pull job file
- Review contract and scope
- Check completion documentation
- Review any prior communications
- Identify sales rep and crew lead

**2.2 Internal Assessment**
Contact relevant team members:
- Sales rep: Customer expectations, promises made
- Crew lead: Work performed, any noted issues
- Office: Communications, scheduling issues

**2.3 Site Visit (if needed)**
For quality/workmanship complaints:
- Schedule inspection within 48 hours
- Document findings with photos
- Assess validity of complaint
- Determine root cause

**2.4 Document Findings**
```
INVESTIGATION NOTES
===================
Date: ___________
Investigator: _________________

COMPLAINT VALID?
□ Yes - Company at fault
□ Partial - Shared responsibility
□ No - Not our responsibility (explain below)

FINDINGS:
_______________________________________
_______________________________________

ROOT CAUSE:
_______________________________________

RECOMMENDED RESOLUTION:
_______________________________________
_______________________________________
```

### 3. Response Based on Finding

**3.1 If Company is at Fault**

**Call Script:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company].
I'm calling about the issue you reported.

First, I want to apologize. You're right, and we
should have done better. Here's what we're going
to do to make it right:

[Explain specific resolution]

I want to make sure you're completely satisfied.
Does this work for you? Is there anything else
I can do?"
```

**Resolution Options:**
- Repair/redo work at no charge
- Partial refund
- Credit toward future services
- Additional warranty extension
- Combination of above

**3.2 If Partial Responsibility**

**Call Script:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company].
I looked into your concern and I want to explain
what I found.

[Explain findings honestly]

While [their portion], we also [our portion].
In the spirit of good customer service, here's
what I'd like to propose:

[Offer reasonable compromise]

What do you think?"
```

**3.3 If Not Our Fault**

**Call Script:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company].
I've thoroughly reviewed your concern, and I want
to walk you through what I found.

[Explain findings with evidence/documentation]

While I understand this isn't the answer you were
hoping for, I want to be honest with you. This
[explain why not covered/our fault].

That said, I don't want to leave you without options.
Here's what I can do: [offer goodwill gesture if
appropriate, or referral to right party]"
```

### 4. Escalation Protocol

**When to Escalate:**
- Customer threatens legal action
- Customer threatens social media/review damage
- High-value customer ($50k+ lifetime)
- Complaint involves safety
- First response not accepted
- Request for manager

**Escalation Path:**
1. Operations Manager
2. Owner/General Manager
3. Legal counsel (if needed)

**Escalation Notification:**
```
ESCALATION NOTICE
=================
Date: ___________
Customer: _________________________
Original Complaint Date: ____________
Escalation Reason:
□ Legal threat
□ Reputation threat
□ High-value customer
□ Safety concern
□ Unresolved after attempt
□ Customer request

Summary:
_______________________________________

Actions Taken:
_______________________________________

Escalated To: _____________ Date: _____
```

### 5. Resolution Documentation

**5.1 Resolution Agreement**
Document what was agreed:
```
COMPLAINT RESOLUTION
====================
Customer: _________________________
Complaint Date: ___________________
Resolution Date: __________________

RESOLUTION AGREED:
□ Repair at no charge
□ Partial refund: $________
□ Full refund: $________
□ Extended warranty: ___ years
□ Credit toward future work: $________
□ No action (complaint invalid)
□ Other: _________________________

Resolution Details:
_______________________________________
_______________________________________

Customer Acceptance:
□ Verbal  □ Written  □ Email

Follow-Up Scheduled: _________________

Resolved By: _____________ Date: ______
```

**5.2 Close Loop with Customer**
After resolution completed:
```
"Hi [Name], I'm following up to make sure everything
was taken care of to your satisfaction. Are you
happy with how we resolved this?

Thank you for giving us the opportunity to make
it right. We truly value your business."
```

### 6. Online Review Responses

**6.1 Negative Review Response**
Always respond publicly, within 24 hours:

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for your feedback. We're sorry your
experience didn't meet expectations.

We take this seriously and would like the
opportunity to make it right. Please contact
[Name] at [Phone/Email] so we can discuss this
directly and find a resolution.

- [Your Name], [Company]
```

**6.2 After Resolution**
Politely request update:
```
"Hi [Name], thank you again for giving us the
chance to resolve this. If you feel we've made
it right, would you consider updating your review
to reflect your current experience? Either way,
we appreciate your business and feedback."
```

### 7. Complaint Analysis

**7.1 Monthly Review**
- Total complaints received
- Categories of complaints
- Resolution time averages
- Resolution costs
- Repeat complaint patterns
- Training needs identified

**7.2 Trend Identification**
Look for patterns:
- Same crew multiple complaints
- Same type of issue recurring
- Process failures
- Communication gaps

**7.3 Improvement Actions**
Based on patterns:
- Update processes
- Additional training
- Equipment/material changes
- Personnel discussions

### 8. Prevention

**Best Practices:**
- Set clear expectations upfront
- Communicate proactively
- Daily job site updates
- Thorough cleanup
- Quality inspections before leaving
- Prompt invoice delivery
- Follow-up after completion
