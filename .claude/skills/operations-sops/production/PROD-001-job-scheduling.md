## Purpose

Ensure efficient scheduling of roofing jobs to maximize crew productivity, meet customer expectations, and optimize profitability.

## Scope

All residential and commercial roofing installations from contract signing through completion.

## Scheduling Principles

1. **Weather First** - Always check extended forecast before scheduling
2. **Crew Capacity** - Match job size to crew capability
3. **Geographic Efficiency** - Cluster jobs by location when possible
4. **Customer Expectations** - Communicate realistic timelines
5. **Material Timing** - Coordinate delivery with start date

## Job Classification

| Type | Typical Duration | Crew Size | Lead Time |
|------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| Repair (small) | 2-4 hours | 2 | 1-3 days |
| Repair (large) | 4-8 hours | 2-3 | 3-5 days |
| Residential Replace | 1-2 days | 4-6 | 5-14 days |
| Large Residential | 2-3 days | 5-7 | 7-14 days |
| Commercial | 3-10 days | 6-10 | 14-30 days |

### 2. Scheduling Process

**2.1 Capacity Assessment**
Check weekly schedule for:
- Available crew(s)
- Equipment availability
- Committed jobs
- Buffer for delays

**2.2 Job Sizing**
Estimate crew-days needed:
```
Squares √ó Complexity Factor √ó Weather Factor = Crew-Days

Complexity Factors:
- Simple roof: 0.15 days/square
- Average roof: 0.20 days/square
- Complex roof: 0.30 days/square

Weather Factors:
- Good conditions: 1.0
- Mixed forecast: 1.2
- Short days (winter): 1.3
```

**2.4 Calendar Booking**
When scheduling:
1. Select date based on crew availability and weather
2. Allow buffer days for weather delays
3. Block material delivery (1-2 days before)
4. Block crew on calendar
5. Schedule dumpster delivery/pickup
6. Schedule any specialty trades

### 5. Week-Before Checklist

**5.1 Operations Review (7 days out)**
- [ ] Confirm crew assignment

- [ ] Check permit status
- [ ] Review weather forecast
- [ ] Confirm customer availability

```
"Hi [Name], [Your Name] from [Company]. Just

```

**5.3 Final Confirmation (1-2 days out)**
- [ ] Final weather check
- [ ] Confirm material delivery
- [ ] Confirm dumpster delivery
- [ ] Send crew lead job packet
- [ ] Text customer reminder

### 6. Day-Before Protocol

**6.1 Weather Decision** (by 4:00 PM)
Check forecast for:
- Rain probability (<30% to proceed)
- Wind speed (<25 mph to proceed)
- Temperature (appropriate for materials)

If postponing:
```
"Hi [Name], [Your Name] from [Company]. Unfortunately,
tomorrow's weather forecast [describe issue] isn't
safe for roofing work. We need to reschedule to
[new date]. I apologize for the inconvenience -
your roof is our priority and we want to do it right.
Does [new date] work for you?"
```

**6.2 Crew Notification**
By 5:00 PM, confirm with crew lead:
- Job address and start time
- Customer name and contact
- Scope of work review
- Material delivery status
- Special instructions
- Safety considerations

### 7. Schedule Changes

**7.1 Customer-Requested Reschedule**
- Accommodate when possible
- First available opening
- No reschedule fee (first time)
- Document reason

**7.2 Company-Initiated Reschedule**
Valid reasons:
- Weather
- Prior job delay
- Material unavailability
- Crew emergency

Protocol:
- Minimum 24-hour notice when possible
- Personal phone call (not just text)
- Apologize and offer next available
- Consider compensation for inconvenience

**7.3 Emergency Rescheduling**
For same-day cancellations:
1. Call customer immediately
2. Explain situation honestly
3. Offer first available reschedule
4. Offer compensation: [Company policy]
5. Follow up with written confirmation

## Schedule Optimization Tips

1. **Monday starts** - Begin large jobs Monday for weather buffer
2. **Friday completions** - Aim to complete jobs before weekend
3. **Geographic clustering** - Schedule nearby jobs consecutively
4. **Crew continuity** - Same crew through job completion
5. **PM repairs** - Schedule small repairs in afternoon slots
