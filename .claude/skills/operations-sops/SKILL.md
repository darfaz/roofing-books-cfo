# Roofing Operations SOPs

Comprehensive Standard Operating Procedures for running a professional roofing company. Well-documented SOPs reduce owner dependency, ensure consistency, enable scaling, and increase business value.

## When to Use This Skill

- Documenting new processes
- Training new employees
- Ensuring operational consistency
- Preparing for business valuation/sale
- Troubleshooting process breakdowns
- Onboarding new team members

## SOP Categories

| Category | Purpose | Documents |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| [Sales](sales/) | Lead-to-close process | Lead handling, estimates, follow-up, closing |
| [Production](production/) | Job execution | Scheduling, installation, punch list |
| [Office](office/) | Administrative functions | Permits, invoicing, collections, filing |
| [Safety](safety/) | Worker safety | Daily briefings, fall protection, emergencies |
| [Quality Control](quality-control/) | Quality assurance | Inspections, callbacks, standards |
| [Customer Service](customer-service/) | Customer experience | Communication, complaints, reviews |

## SOP Document Format

Each SOP follows a standard format for consistency:

```
SOP TITLE
=========
Document #: [Department]-[Number]
Version: [X.X]
Effective Date: [Date]
Last Reviewed: [Date]
Owner: [Role responsible]

PURPOSE
-------
Why this procedure exists and what it accomplishes.

SCOPE
-----
Who this applies to and when it should be followed.

RESPONSIBILITIES
----------------
Who does what in this process.

PROCEDURE
---------
Step-by-step instructions with decision points.

RELATED DOCUMENTS
-----------------
Forms, checklists, and other SOPs referenced.

REVISION HISTORY
----------------
| Version | Date | Changes | Author |
```

## Implementation Guide

### Phase 1: Document Current State
1. Observe current processes
2. Interview team members
3. Document "as-is" procedures
4. Identify gaps and inconsistencies

### Phase 2: Standardize
1. Define best practices
2. Create draft SOPs
3. Review with team
4. Finalize and distribute

### Phase 3: Train
1. Roll out to team
2. Conduct training sessions
3. Document training completion
4. Monitor compliance

### Phase 4: Maintain
1. Regular review schedule (quarterly)
2. Update as needed
3. Version control
4. Continuous improvement
