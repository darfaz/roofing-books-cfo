# Insurance Claims Assistant

Help roofing contractors navigate insurance claims with professional documentation, effective supplements, and clear adjuster communication.

## When to Use This Skill

- Documenting storm damage (hail, wind, fallen trees)
- Writing supplement requests for additional work
- Preparing for insurance adjuster meetings
- Creating photo documentation checklists
- Communicating with insurance carriers
- Reviewing Xactimate estimates

## Insurance Claim Process Overview

### Phase 1: Initial Documentation
1. **First Contact** - Document date/time of storm and customer call
2. **Property Inspection** - Systematic damage documentation
3. **Photo Evidence** - Comprehensive photo protocol
4. **Damage Report** - Written assessment for adjuster

### Phase 2: Adjuster Meeting
1. **Preparation** - Organize documentation
2. **Roof Walk** - Guide adjuster through damage
3. **Agreement** - Document scope of work approved
4. **Disagreement** - Request re-inspection if needed

### Phase 3: Supplement Process
1. **Hidden Damage** - Document discovered issues
2. **Code Upgrades** - Required compliance items
3. **Supplement Request** - Formal documentation
4. **Negotiation** - Professional follow-up

## Photo Documentation Protocol

### Required Photos (Minimum)

**Property Overview**
- [ ] Front of property with address visible
- [ ] All 4 sides of property
- [ ] Street view showing neighboring properties

**Roof Overview**
- [ ] Each roof slope/plane
- [ ] Overall roof condition
- [ ] Satellite view printout with measurements

**Damage Documentation**
- [ ] Wide shot showing damage location
- [ ] Medium shot with reference point
- [ ] Close-up of specific damage
- [ ] Chalk circle around damage (where appropriate)
- [ ] Measurement reference (tape measure, coin)

**Soft Metals Test**
- [ ] Gutters with hail strikes
- [ ] Downspouts with damage
- [ ] Roof vents and flashings
- [ ] AC unit damage
- [ ] Window screens

**Collateral Damage**
- [ ] Fence damage
- [ ] Siding damage
- [ ] Window damage
- [ ] Deck/patio damage

## Storm Damage Terminology

### Hail Damage Indicators
- **Granule displacement** - Shiny spots where granules are knocked off
- **Bruising/Soft spots** - Compromised mat under granules
- **Cracking** - Fractured shingle surface
- **Exposed fiberglass** - Severe granule loss exposing mat

### Wind Damage Indicators
- **Creasing** - Horizontal crease from lifting
- **Lifted tabs** - Unsealed or torn tabs
- **Missing shingles** - Complete loss
- **Curling** - Edges turned up from wind stress

## Best Practices

1. **Over-document** - More photos are always better
2. **Be specific** - Use exact measurements and locations
3. **Reference codes** - Cite building codes for required items
4. **Stay professional** - Maintain cordial adjuster relationships
5. **Follow up** - Persistent, polite follow-up gets results
6. **Document everything** - Keep records of all communications
